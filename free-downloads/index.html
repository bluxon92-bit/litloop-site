A Litloop Short Classics Anthology

Short stories from the golden age of science fiction

Between roughly 1935 and the early 1960s, a generation of writers worked out almost everything science fiction would go on to be — first contact, robot ethics, simulated reality, surveillance dystopia, faster-than-light travel, post-scarcity economics, the unthinkable.

This collection gathers ten of the cleanest, sharpest examples in the public domain — Asimov, Bradbury, Pohl, Laumer, plus an original new story from Litloop's own Ben Luxon.
