Essential Biography & Memoir

Why biography matters: A great biography does what fiction claims to — it renders a complete human life, with all its contradictions. Robert Caro's Lyndon Johnson series is the supreme example in the form.

For political biography: Caro's The Path to Power is where to start. For something more recent, Barack Obama's A Promised Land is unusually candid for a presidential memoir.

For literary memoir: Mary Karr's The Liar's Club defines the modern memoir. Maggie Nelson's The Argonauts and Carmen Maria Machado's In the Dream House show where the form is going.

For accessible entry points: Matthew Crawford's The World Beyond Your Head, Michelle Obama's Becoming, and Tara Westover's Educated are all exceptional and reach wide audiences.
