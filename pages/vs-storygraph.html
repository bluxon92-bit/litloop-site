Litloop vs StoryGraph

Both are reading trackers.
They do different things.

StoryGraph is brilliant for reading data and mood-based discovery. Litloop adds the layer StoryGraph leaves out: real conversations and connections with the people you actually want to talk books with.

Where StoryGraph excels

StoryGraph is great if you care about stats.

StoryGraph built a loyal following for real reasons, it's a clean, independent reading tracker with the best stats and mood-based discovery in the space. If you care about reading data, it's hard to beat.

Deep reading stats

Monthly breakdowns, average ratings, reading pace, genre splits. The data goes further than most apps dare.

Mood-based discovery

Find books by how you want to feel while reading them, not just genre. It's a genuinely clever way to discover what to read next.

Independent & ad-free

No Amazon ownership. No ads. StoryGraph is built by readers for readers, and it shows.

StoryGraph asks one question. Litloop asks another.

StoryGraph is a deliberately personal reading tracker. The social features are intentionally minimal. The focus is on your reading, your data, your recommendations from their algorithm.

Litloop starts from a different place entirely. The question isn't "what should you read next?" — it's "who do you want to talk about it with?" That shapes everything.

StoryGraph asks

"What does your reading data tell you?"

Litloop asks

"Who do you want to share it with?"

Where Litloop does things differently

The social layer StoryGraph doesn't have.

Litloop does all the things a great reading tracker should do, then adds the bit that makes it worth opening every day.

01

Friends-first feed

Instead of algorithm-driven suggestions, Litloop shows you what the actual people in your life are reading right now. Real taste from real people you trust.

02

Personal recommendations

Your friends can recommend books to you with a personal note explaining exactly why they thought of you. Better than any algorithm because they actually know you.

03

Private book conversations

Start a private chat about any book with one friend or a whole group. Say what you really think without performing for strangers.
